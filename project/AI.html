​So what is AI?

​Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are designed to think and act like humans. These machines are programmed to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and language translation. The goal of AI is to create systems that can perform tasks that normally require human intelligence and can learn from experience.

​How will AI help humanity?

​AI has the potential to greatly benefit society in many ways, including:
Healthcare: AI can help doctors diagnose diseases more accurately, personalize treatment plans, and improve patient outcomes.
Transportation: AI can make self-driving cars a reality, reducing traffic accidents and improving road safety.
Education: AI can provide personalized learning experiences and improve access to education for people around the world.
Environment: AI can help monitor and protect the environment, predict natural disasters, and support sustainable development.
Manufacturing: AI can increase efficiency, reduce waste, and improve product quality in manufacturing processes.
Finance: AI can help detect and prevent fraud, reduce risk, and improve the speed and accuracy of financial transactions.
Agriculture: AI can help farmers optimize their operations, improve crop yields, and reduce the impact of farming on the environment.
However, it's important to consider that AI can also have unintended consequences, such as job displacement and biased decision making, so careful development and regulation is necessary to ensure its benefits are maximized and its negative impacts minimized.

TYPES OF AI

​There are several types of AI, including: 

​Reactive Machines

​These are the simplest type of AI systems, which can only react to the environment without having any memory. An example is Deep Blue, the computer that defeated chess Grandmaster Garry Kasparov in 1997.

​Limited Memory

​These AI systems can store and recall past experiences to make informed decisions. Examples include self-driving cars that use sensors and cameras to remember the roads and traffic conditions.

​Theory of Mind

​This type of AI refers to machines that can understand human emotions and intentions. It is still a developing field and has yet to be fully realized.

​Self-Aware

​This is the most advanced form of AI, where machines have a sense of consciousness and self-awareness. This type of AI is still purely fictional and has not yet been achieved.

​Narrow AI

​Also known as weak AI, narrow AI refers to AI systems that are designed to perform a single task, such as image recognition or speech recognition.

​General AI

​: Also known as strong AI, this refers to AI systems that can perform a wide range of tasks and display human-like intelligence. This type of AI is still a work in progress and has yet to be achieved.

​A little about AI and the model we will use:
Deep learning is a subset of machine learning that uses artificial neural networks with multiple layers, known as deep neural networks, to analyze and process data. It is based on the idea that a computer system can learn to perform tasks without being specifically programmed to do so, by training on large amounts of data and adjusting the parameters of the model to minimize the error in its predictions.
Deep learning models consist of layers of artificial neurons, which were inspired by the structure of the human brain. These layers are trained to extract features from the input data, and then use those features to make predictions or decisions. The number of layers in a deep learning model can vary, but models with more layers are called deeper models and are capable of learning more complex representations of the input data.
The most common types of deep learning models are feed forward neural networks, convolutional neural networks, and recurrent neural networks. These models can be used for a wide variety of applications, such as image and speech recognition, natural language processing, and decision making.
Deep learning models can be trained on a variety of tasks, including supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning. In supervised learning, the model is trained to predict a target variable based on input data, using a labeled dataset. In unsupervised learning, the model is trained to discover patterns in the input data without labeled targets. In reinforcement learning, the model learns to make decisions by interacting with an environment and receiving feedback in the form of rewards or punishments.
